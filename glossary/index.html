Jurassic Park IV: The Judas Strain - Glossary

character

Adrien Joyce
Also: Joyce, Colonel, Colonel Joyce
Grendel International's chief of operations and the Baron von Drax's right hand. A tailored, fencer-trained mercenary with a half-smile and a long résumé of African and Latin American gun-running. He recruits Nick into the program and runs the day-to-day command of the raptor missions.
Baron Herman von Drax
Also: von Drax, Baron, Baron von Drax, the Baron, Herman von Drax
The twenty-third Baron von Drax, principal stockholder and CEO of the Grendel International Corporation. A heavy florid man in bespoke burgundy who carries the conviction of a bloodline bred for war. He has personally contributed the human DNA spliced into the Deinonychus draxi line.
Bertrand Chartiers
Chairman of the Duhamel Group, a French conglomerate with substantial holdings in former colonies. Two of his employees have already been killed during ransom negotiations before his daughter Isabel is taken in Tangier.
Dennis Nedry
Also: Nedry
A former Jurassic Park employee who stole a Barbasol can full of dinosaur embryos and vanished on the island during the original incident. His skeletal remains are found in a vine-choked 4x4 by Nick, still in the act of trying to leave the seat.
Isabel Chartiers
Also: Isabel
The ten-year-old daughter of a French industrialist, held for ransom in a Tangier warehouse by the so-called North African Liberation Front. She rides a small bay pony in the briefing photograph; a violet hand-knit sweater belonging to her becomes the scent-binding object for the raptor squad.
Jeb Overton
Also: Overton, Captain Overton, Major Overton
A bionic-legged former captain of Nick's old unit, now wearing a hummingbird-patched uniform as security chief at Hammond's aviary. He recruits Nick for the Nublar operation and serves as Nick's outside contact and runner during the Grendel job.
John Hammond
Also: Hammond, Mr. Hammond
The frail, cane-leaning founder of the original Jurassic Park, now confined to a private coastal aviary by family decree. Declared incompetent and surrounded by minders, he bankrolls a covert effort to retrieve the Nedry can from Isla Nublar through the trust that funds the aviary.
Kroner
Joyce's driver and personal aide at the castle. Quiet, watchful, and always near the wheel of a Land Rover or leaning against a courtyard wall with an unsmoked cigarette, he doubles as a low-key minder for Nick and Maya whenever they leave a room together.
Maya Lundberg
Also: Maya, Miss Lundberg, Lundberg
Grendel International's head of Behavioral Modification. Holds a doctorate in behavioral sciences with field experience training wolves in Saskatchewan and big cats in a circus ring. She runs the cage work with the X-series raptors and is one of the few employees willing to push back against Joyce and the Baron.
Nick Harris
Also: Nick, Harris, Mr. Harris
A former Navy SEAL turned freelance operator, hired by John Hammond to retrieve a stolen can of dinosaur DNA from Isla Nublar. Tactically capable and internally cynical, with a history of contract work in places he is not supposed to talk about. After being captured by Grendel, he becomes the operator-trainer for the X-series raptor program.
Pepe Aguilar
Also: Aguilar
A Latin American narco-lord who controls a large share of the heroin and cocaine moving out of the continent from his fortified Cuchibamba compound. He keeps a private army, a fleet of cargo planes, and several dozen kidnapped hostages, including the President's favorite niece.
Sherman Fosdick
Also: Sherman, Fosdick
Grendel's chief of Applied Genetics. A soft, hunched young man in thick-rimmed glasses and a rumpled lab coat who narrates his own work compulsively. He designs the neuro-regulator implants, runs the radio control consoles, and is responsible for the cross-species splices in the X-series.
Zeiss
A junior Grendel handler with a small affected mustache, given to the cattle prod and the harness. He treats the raptors as discipline cases rather than animals and serves Joyce as muscle and a sidearm-carrying minder when Nick is on a short leash.

raptor

Achilles
Also: X-5
The smallest and fastest of the X-series, a juvenile of a species that is not supposed to have juveniles. Quickest to flare and quickest to break formation, with an uneven temper that makes him the squad's first scout across minefields and tripwires.
Hector
One of the five named X-series Deinonychus draxi, drilled in the valley training ground alongside Spartacus and the others. Holds a flank position in the squad's wedge formation and tends to hold a scent longer than his packmates.
Orestes
One of the five named X-series Deinonychus draxi. A nervous, taking-the-sleeve type in training; pairs with Perseus on the center line of the breach formation and tends to lead through doorways.
Perseus
One of the five named X-series Deinonychus draxi. A center-line breacher in the assault wedge, paired most often with Orestes during interior takedowns.
Spartacus
Also: X-1
The alpha male of the X-series Deinonychus draxi line, eight feet tall and the largest of the five. His eyes hold a deeper red than the others and his attention does not let go once it settles. He counts doors, men, and the dominant hands at the control console, filing every observation.

concept

Barbasol can
Also: aerosol can, the can
A muck-stained shaving cream can with a welded false bottom, used by Dennis Nedry to smuggle eight ampoules of frozen dinosaur embryos off Isla Nublar. Recovered by Nick from a jungle streambed fifteen years later; the entire plot revolves around who ends up holding it.
Conversation of death
Maya's framing for the eye-contact understanding that passes between a wolf and the member of the herd it has chosen. The herd keeps grazing because they know it is not their turn. She uses the same look to walk into and out of the X-series cage alive.
Daddy drug
Also: insulin dependency
The chemical leash Sherman built into the X-series at the genetic level. Their bodies manufacture insulin but cannot secrete it without a Grendel-supplied stimulant; an animal cut off from the company's signal lasts roughly an hour before it crashes.
Judas strain
Hammond's proposed solution to the global dinosaur problem: an influx of young, aggressive, sexually attractive but reproductively neutered females released into the wild population to crash the birth rate. Outlawed by the United Nations along with the mining of amber.
Neuro-regulator implant
Also: regulator box, neuro-implant, implants
A small plastic-and-wire box bolted into the skull of each X-series raptor and a lighter version on the trained Spitters. Allows Sherman to push hormones and neuropeptides into the animal's bloodstream by radio signal, including pain, fatigue, bliss, and sedation.
Oxytocin scent-binding
Maya's training protocol for the Tangier extraction: dose the raptors with oxytocin while letting them sniff a child's worn sweater, fixing parental pair-bond chemistry onto her individual scent. The chemistry holds long enough to walk Isabel out of a room full of dead kidnappers.
Pheromone armband
A thick red tactical armband with a sewn-in scent-pebble, issued to handlers working near the X-series. The raptors are trained to avoid the molecule it releases, which to a human nose smells like nothing at all.
X-series
Also: X-1, X-2, X-3, X-4, X-5
Grendel International's program code for its five bio-engineered Deinonychus draxi. Each unit carries a fluorescent number sprayed on the flank and a regulator box bolted into the skull. Nick formally retires the numbers and renames the line Spartacus, Hector, Achilles, Perseus, and Orestes.

creature

Ankylosaurus
Also: Ankylosaur
An armored herbivore the size of a small car, raised by Grendel from an Isla Nublar embryo and kept in a stone pit beneath the castle. Hammers its bowling-ball tail-club into walls in a slow steady rhythm and tends to wander off into any doorway with ferns on the other side.
Casper
One of two trained Spitters attached to the Grendel strike team for the Cuchibamba run. Smaller and lower than the raptors, used for surveillance-camera blinding and tight-space access ahead of the main wedge. Named by Nick alongside Pollux.
Compsognathus
Also: Compy, Compies
A small, chicken-sized predator with yellow eyes and far too many teeth for a face that small. Hunts in squeaking packs, individually no harder to handle than a Jack Russell terrier and collectively capable of stripping a man.
Deinonychus draxi
Also: Deinonychus
The Grendel-engineered raptor sub-species at the heart of the X-series program. Eight feet tall, mottled black-to-green chameleon scales, ruby eyes, and a hooked second-toe sickle. Built on a Deinonychus base spliced with Ornitholestes, domestic dog, and a small section of Baron von Drax's own DNA.
Excavaraptor
Also: Excavaraptors, Digger, Diggers
Eight feet of black-scaled, ruby-eyed muscle on bow legs, with oversized digging claws on each forearm. Lives in tunnel colonies under the surface of Isla Nublar and erupts vertically out of the ground beneath unsuspecting prey, the dirt going somewhere a man cannot see.
Kronosaurus
A submarine-sized marine reptile patrolling the waters off Isla Nublar. Surfaces in a grey wedge that becomes a head the size of a small car, with jaws wider than a floatplane's cabin. It can take a tail section off an aircraft in a single tear.
Ornitholestes
An egg-stealing dinosaur whose DNA Sherman has spliced into the Deinonychus draxi genome. Contributes the wrong-long forelimbs and the over-articulated, almost finger-like claws that let the X-series manipulate latches, boxes, and one another.
Pollux
The second of the two trained Spitters on the Grendel strike team. Hangs from branches by his tail to ambush perimeter sentries and dives under tripwires the raptors cannot cross. Named by Nick alongside Casper.
Pterosaur
Also: Pterosaurs
A flock of leathery-winged flying reptiles with crocodilian snouts and far too many teeth. They appear in coordinated phalanxes too disciplined to be bird flocks, buzzing American Little League fields and snatching meat off plates and children off the basepaths.
Spitter
Also: Spitters, Chlamydosaurus sputori, Frilled lizard
A frilled, dog-sized predator that hawks a palm-sized plug of black acidic mucus from a ratcheting throat-mantle. Wild-type animals burrow underground on Isla Nublar; a trained pair, Casper and Pollux, has been added to the Grendel strike package.

location

Cuchibamba
The deep-jungle compound of narco-lord Pepe Aguilar, complete with checkpoints, barracks, a state-of-the-art drug lab, and a hacienda-style central residence. The target of the Grendel strike team's full-team marketing demonstration.
Grendel Castle
Also: the castle, Swiss castle
A fifteenth-century mountainside fortress in a Swiss canton, headquarters of Grendel International. Above ground it runs as a tourist attraction with yodelers and cable cars; beneath it sits a multi-level cavern containing the cold-storage genome lab and the X-series enclosures.
Hammond's Aviary
Also: the aviary
A private netted dome on the American coast where John Hammond is held under genteel house-arrest by a sturdy nurse. Funded through a trust he set up before his legal troubles, its board includes some of the finest geneticists in the world.
Isla Nublar
Also: Nublar
The original Jurassic Park island, now a rotting tropical jungle of fallen lamp posts, torn chain-link, and overgrown service roads. Officially under Grendel International stewardship and declared near-clean of dinosaurs; in practice still riddled with Diggers, Spitters, Compies, and worse in the surf.
Little League field
A suburban American ballpark on a hot Saturday afternoon where the prologue's pterosaur attack opens the story. Aluminum bleachers, a Coleman cooler, a Video Enthusiast with four thousand dollars of camera, and a backstop that ends the day in shreds.
Rodrigo's
A small Caribbean dive-bar in a tourist town, run by an old oyster-shucker. Its kitchen Coca-Cola cooler, stenciled HIELO, becomes the hiding place for the genuine Barbasol can while Nick is on the run from Grendel.
Tangier waterfront
Also: Tangier
A half-mile square of decaying brick, plaster, and corrugated tin along the Moroccan docks, where Isabel Chartiers is held in a second-floor warehouse office. The smell of olive oil, wet rope, and harbor fuel layers under everything that happens there.

organization

Grendel International
Also: Grendel, Grendel Corporation, Grendel International Corporation, Grendel Corp.
A Swiss multinational headed by Baron Herman von Drax, public face of the company that took possession of Isla Nublar after the original park collapsed. Fronts as a producer of insect-resistant rye, hops, barley, and pre-sliced cheese; underneath, it runs a bio-engineered weapons program for sale to the highest bidder.
North African Liberation Front
The cell holding Isabel Chartiers in Tangier, presented as more criminal than political. Six or seven men with Russian rifles and Algerian accents, conspicuously convenient in their composition.
